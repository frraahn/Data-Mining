# 📊 Data Mining (DM)

Welcome to my repository for the Data Mining course (`SECP2753`). This space serves as a portfolio of my work in data preprocessing, machine learning model development, and extracting actionable business insights from complex health datasets.

---

## 📖 Course Overview
This course explores the entire Knowledge Discovery in Databases (KDD) process. Through hands-on assignments and projects, I learned how to clean messy real-world data, handle unbalanced datasets, and apply supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms to transform raw data into valuable decision-making insights.

Key themes include data preprocessing, classification modeling, clustering analysis, and clinical insight segmentation.

---

## 📂 Repository Structure & Project Breakdown

### 🔹 Course Projects
* **Project 1 (`/SECP2753-02 DATA MINING PROJECT 1.pdf`)**: A comprehensive predictive analytics project. Implemented and compared three major classification models—**Decision Tree**, **Naive Bayes**, and **Support Vector Machine (SVM)**—to evaluate clinical risk levels and predict patient attributes based on vital biomarkers.
* **Project 2 (`/group4_project2.pdf`)**: Focused on unsupervised learning. Implemented **Clustering Algorithms** to achieve clinical risk segmentation, analyzing the relationships between BMI, Cholesterol, HbA1c, and Fasting Glucose to discover natural health groupings within the data.

### 🔹 Assignments
* **Assignment 1 (`/ASSIGNMENT 1 DATA MINING (SECP2753).pdf`)**: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) focusing on a rich diabetes dataset. Investigated data characteristics, demographics, and the minimal vs. major impacts of lifestyle factors (like smoking and physical activity) on patient health outcomes.

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Concepts Covered
* **Languages & Tools:** Python, Data Mining Frameworks
* **Algorithms:** Decision Trees, Naive Bayes, Support Vector Machines (SVM), K-Means/Hierarchical Clustering.
* **Concepts:** Data Preprocessing (Handling missing entries, Categorical Encoding), Stratified Train/Test Splits, Model Evaluation (Sensitivity vs. Specificity), and Business Intelligence.

---

### 💡 Course Reflection & Key Takeaways
This course completely shifted my perspective on how raw data is transformed into meaningful intelligence. Working through the datasets taught me that real-world data is incredibly messy, and I realized that the core strength of data mining lies heavily in the preprocessing and data cleaning stages. Building classification models like Decision Trees, Naive Bayes, and SVMs to predict health markers showed me how to balance statistical accuracy with real-world clinical interpretability. Ultimately, discovering natural patient groupings through clustering algorithms proved that data mining is not just an abstract academic exercise, but a powerful asset for generating business intelligence and driving predictive clinical strategies.
